I started making explainer videos on machine learning in 2019 when I was a confused sophomore. It was partly due to the fact that I am a firm believer in the Fynman technique (not the integral calculus one!) and partly due to the influence of OG science YouTubers (Derek Muller, Brady Haran, Matt Parker, and many more). Had you suggested back then that one of my videos would rack up over a million views, I'd have laughed so hard. The initial videos were, to put it simply, unwatchable. Thankfully, I wasn't aware of that so I kept going. Gradually, the quality improved, and at some point, I came across Manim (a Python animation library created by none other than Grant Sanderson, aka 3B1B). Suddenly (well, ignoring the steep learning curve) I was able to create visually pleasing animations! I tried to put my heart and soul into every video; however, below are the videos I had the most fun making! Please visit my YouTube channel to enjoy more :)
